Three Rules That Keep It Honest

01

The Run Wins

Hard lift vs. key run collision, or you're wiped? Prioritise the run. Strength holds on 3–4 lifts a week. Aerobic progression doesn't forgive skipped long runs.

02

Easy Stays Easy

Sunday run and Sunday bike both stay conversational. The moment either goes hard, recovery is gone and you've stacked a third hard day onto the long run.

03

Clear The Legs

Friday is upper body — safe before Saturday's long run. The watch point is Thursday Lower 2: pull it back on every big-mileage week.

Maintenance Lifting — when flagged

Preserve strength without burying the legs: 3–4 sets × 3–5 reps, 2–3 in reserve, no grinding. No high-rep burnout, no leg finishers. Keeps the strength stimulus, skips the soreness + glycogen hit that wrecks the next run.

Why This Lineup

Anti-Movement First

Plank, dead bug, hollow, side plank train the core to resist motion — exactly the strength that holds running form late in a long run and stabilises you on the bike.

Frequency > Brutality

Abs recover fast. Small and daily beats occasional smash sessions. You already hit the core hard via squats & deadlifts, so this stays moderate by design.

Progress, Don't Extend

When 5 min feels easy, add difficulty, not time: weighted plank, overhead-loaded dead bug, full-leg hollow, side-plank hip dips, slower bicycles.
